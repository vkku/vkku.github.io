# Rishikesh to Chopta and Tungnath: A Himalayan Odyssey

Embarking on a journey from the spiritual heart of Rishikesh to the serene heights of Chopta and the divine Tungnath, this trek offers a blend of adventure, spirituality, and breathtaking landscapes.

## Itinerary Overview

| Day | Activity | Highlights |
|-----|----------|------------|
| Day 1 | Rishikesh to Chopta | Scenic drive through forests |
| Day 2 | Chopta Exploration | Local villages, meadows |
| Day 3 | Trek to Tungnath | Highest Shiva temple, panoramic views |
| Day 4 | Return Journey | Reflection and departure |

## Day 1: Arrival in Chopta

After a peaceful drive from Rishikesh, we arrived in Chopta, a picturesque village nestled in the Garhwal Himalayas.

![Chopta Meadows](/static/chopta/meadows.jpg)
*Chopta's lush green meadows*

### Accommodation
We stayed in a cozy homestay with stunning views of the surrounding peaks.

## Day 2: Exploring Chopta

Chopta is known as the "Mini Switzerland" of India. We spent the day wandering through the meadows and interacting with locals.

## Day 3: Ascent to Tungnath

The trek to Tungnath (3,680m) is moderate but rewarding. The temple, dedicated to Lord Shiva, offers spiritual solace amidst the mountains.

### Route Details
- Distance: 4km one way
- Duration: 2-3 hours
- Difficulty: Moderate

![Tungnath Temple](/static/tungnath/temple.jpg)
*Tungnath Temple - Highest Shiva temple in the world*

## Travel Tips

- **Best Time to Visit**: April to June, September to November
- **Permits**: No special permits required for Chopta-Tungnath
- **Packing Essentials**: Warm clothes, sturdy shoes, rain gear
- **Health Precautions**: Acclimatize to altitude, carry basic medicines
